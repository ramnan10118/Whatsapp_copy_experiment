**Objective:** CTR ‚Üí contest registration / participation

### Example 1
**Label:** HIGH CTR ‚Äî BEST SINGLE PERFORMER
**CTR:** 1.14% | **Cohort:** ~3,300 | **Time:** 9‚Äì11 AM
**Tone:** Exclusivity / Utility framing

**Header:** Important: For ACKO customers
**Body:** We're running a contest where you can win a brand NEW CAR! Tap here to register.
**CTA:** Tap here to register

**Why it worked:**
- "Important:" signals utility, not a promotional message ‚Äî bypasses the "this is marketing" filter in users' minds and earns attention as if it's a service notification
- "For ACKO customers" creates immediate exclusivity and personalisation ‚Äî this feels sent specifically to them, not mass broadcast
- Body is direct: "you can win" not "you might win" ‚Äî confident, not hedged
- Morning slot amplifies the exclusivity frame ‚Äî people scan 9‚Äì11 AM notifications for things that feel personally important
- Note: Same message dropped to 0.80% CTR in afternoon batch ‚Äî this framing is particularly strong in morning

**CTR:** 1.03% (afternoon) | 0.80% (morning) | **Cohort:** ~87,000 (PM) / ~3,200 (AM) | **Time:** Both slots

**Header:** FREE CAR, no joke!
**Body:** We are giving away a brand new car absolutely FREE! To participate, tap and wishlist your favourite cars.

**Why it worked:**

- Key lesson: state the prize clearly and handle skepticism in the same line
